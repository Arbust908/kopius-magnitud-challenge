# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Earthquake hobbyists and enthusiasts who want to explore recent seismic activity without a professional GIS workflow. They check the app on phones first, often casually after hearing about an event or feeling a tremor, and also use desktop when they want a wider map view.

## Product Purpose

Earthquake Explorer helps users filter recent USGS earthquake data and understand where events happened, how strong they were, and when they occurred. Success means a user can open the app, adjust a few practical filters, scan the map, and inspect an event without feeling like they are using enterprise software.

## Brand Personality

Clean, practical, tactile. The interface should feel like a crafted field instrument for curious people: focused, calm, and approachable, with enough visual depth to feel physical on mobile screens.

## Anti-references

Do not make it look like a giant corporate dashboard, SaaS admin panel, generic AI-generated interface, or novelty disaster app. Avoid pure black, pure white, generic blue-gray slabs, overused gradient hero aesthetics, and decorative glass effects.

## Design Principles

- Mobile-first control surface: the filters should feel designed for thumbs and small screens before desktop.
- Map remains primary: UI chrome supports exploration and never competes with earthquake geography.
- Tactile but restrained: use depth, rounded forms, and soft contrast with intent, not decoration.
- Practical curiosity: show data plainly, with enough personality to invite exploration.
- Respect ambient preference: dark and light modes should follow the user's system setting and both feel complete.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Default to user color-scheme preference, support both light and dark modes, maintain readable contrast, avoid pure black or pure white, and keep the interface usable on phone and desktop. Use color as an aid, not the only signal, especially for magnitude and status states.
